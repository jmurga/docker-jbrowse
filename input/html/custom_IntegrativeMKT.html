McDonald and Kreitman tests (MKT, McDonald and Kreitman 1991) have been extensively used to detect the signature of natural selection at the molecular level. The MKT compares the amount of variation within a species to the divergence between species at two types of sites: putatively neutral sites (typically synonymous coding sites) are used as the reference to detect selection at the other type of site (typically nonsynonymous coding sites). The MKT can be generalized to any two types of sites provided that one of them is assumed to evolve neutrally and that both types of sites are closely linked in the genome (Fu and Li 1993, Andolfatto 2005). If mutations are either strongly deleterious (and thus do not segregate in the population) or neutral, Psel/Pneu = Dsel/Dneu, where P is the number of segregating polymorphisms and D is the number of divergent substitutions, and sel and neu denote putative selected and neutral sites, respectively. Significant departures from equality (tested with a Fisher Exact Test from a 2x2 contingency table) indicate the segregation of slightly deleterious polymorphisms (Psel/Pneu > Dsel/Dneu) or the selection for adaptive variants (Dsel/Dneu > Psel/Pneu). The fraction of adaptive fixations, α, is estimated from 1 - (Psel/Pneu)(Dneu/Dsel) (Smith and Eyre-Walker 2002). α can be equivalently estimated using π and K with the expression 1 - (πsel/πneu)(Kneu/Ksel) (Sella et al. 2009). α ranges from -Inf to 1, values greater than 0 indicating the fraction of adaptive fixations. Direction of Selection (DoS) is a similar statistic that is defined as Dsel/(Dsel + Dneu) - Psel/(Psel+Pneu) and ranges from -1 to 1. DoS is positive when there is evidence of adaptive selection, zero if there is only neutral evolution, and negative when weakly deleterious mutations are segregating.

Adaptive mutations and weakly deleterious selection act in opposite directions on the MKT, so both will be underestimated when the two selection regimes occur at the same time. Mackay et al. (2012) developed an integrative new framework for the MKT in which the fraction of sites undergoing different regimes of selection can be estimated. They separated Psel into the number of neutral variants (Psel_neutral) and the number of weakly deleterious variants (Psel_weak) by incorporating information of the Minor Allele Frequency (MAF) of the segregating sites and estimated the fractions listed in Table 1.

The standard and integrative MKTs were applied to all annotated human coding regions in RefSeq. Each site in the coding gene region was assigned one of the following classes: 4-fold degenerate synonymous site, 0-fold nondegenerate nonsynonymous site, 5' UTR, 3' UTR, intron, and flanking intergenic region (+/-500bp regions flanking the gene). All annotated transcripts for any gene overlapping a coding region were taken into account in order to classify sites, following the order Intergenic > 4-fold > intron > 3'UTR > 5'UTR > 0-fold, so e.g. a site annotated as intronic according to the transcript being analyzed but 0-fold according to another transcript overlapping the region would be classified as 0-fold. Then, using 4-fold sites as neutral sites, independent MKTs were performed for the other classes of sites.
